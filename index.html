Course Description: This course introduces the use of the Lean 4 proof assistant to formalize concepts and results from theoretical computer science and mathematics. The first part of the course covers the fundamentals of Lean 4, including its logical foundations, core syntax, and proof tactics, through guided exercises and examples. Students will then form small groups and undertake a semester-long formalization project on a selected topic, such as automata theory, computability, complexity theory, or combinatorics. The course emphasizes hands-on engagement with Lean and aims to provide students with practical experience in mechanized reasoning and formal proof development.

Some resources that could be useful for the course:
  • Y. Filmus' course webpage Proving theorems in Lean.
  • S. Gadgil's course webpage Proofs and Programs, an introduction to interactive theorem proving using Lean.
  • H. Macbeth's book The Mechanics of Proof.
  • J. Avigad and P. Massot's book Mathematics in Lean.
  • EPFL Lean mini course, material is adapted from Mathematics in Lean, and from the Lean for the Curious Mathematician 2023 workshop.
  • Search engine for lean4, Loodle!
  • Documentation for mathlib4, a user maintained library for Lean containing a large collection of formalized mathematics.
  • Lean community Zulip channel for asking questions and discussing Lean-related topics.
